Preventing Abalone Overfishing

Who are we?

We are an organization that strives to prevent the overfishing of the abalone sea snail. We do this by using cutting edge, predictive Artificial Intelligence (A.I.) technologies to help abalone fishermen determine wether or not their catch meets the age requirements for capture.

Our Mission!

The abalone snail has been overfished to the verge of extinction. However, by using Automated Machine Learning on AWS, we hope to save the species by helping fishermen determine the correct age to return them back into the wild.

What is an Abalone?

The abalone (Haliotis) was once common in kelp forests in parts of Australia, United Kingdom, South Africa, and has been harvested by humans for around 10,000 years.

It plays an important role in the kelp forest ecosystem, competing with other algal grazers such as sea urchins and keeping their numbers under control.
